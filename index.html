Project Writeup

1. What have you done so far?

We have selected our dataset — the Spotify Tracks Dataset from Kaggle, containing over 114,000 tracks with 21 audio and metadata features including popularity, danceability, energy, valence, tempo, and genre. We performed exploratory data analysis in Python using pandas and matplotlib, which revealed that most songs cluster between popularity scores of 30–50, while a significant spike exists at score 0 (songs with essentially no streams). We built five static visualizations covering popularity distribution, genre rankings, danceability scatter plots, a radar chart of audio features by genre, and a comparison of explicit vs. clean track popularity. For this initial prototype, we have implemented an interactive D3.js scatter plot that allows users to filter tracks by genre and hover over individual songs to see their name, artist, and audio features — establishing the core interactive structure of our final explorable explanation.

2. What will be the most challenging part of your project to design and why?

The most challenging aspect will be designing the scrollytelling narrative structure that smoothly connects multiple visualizations into a coherent, story-driven experience. Unlike a simple dashboard, an explorable explanation requires each section to build naturally on the previous one, guiding the reader through a data-driven story without overwhelming them with too much information at once. We also anticipate challenges in rendering performance, since our dataset contains 114,000 rows and rendering all points in D3 simultaneously risks making the page slow or unresponsive — we will need to implement smart sampling or aggregation strategies. Finally, designing accessible and intuitive interactions (such as genre filtering, sliders, and animated transitions) that feel natural to a non-technical audience will require careful iteration and user-centered design thinking.
